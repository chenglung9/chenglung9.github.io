About Me

I am currently an operations research consultant working in the Operations Research and Advanced Analytics team at American Airlines (ORAA). Prior joining to ORAA, I receive my Ph.D. degree at University of Central Florida in operations research under the supervision of Dr.Vladimir Boginski. I also have a master degree in operations research from the Ohio State University, a master degree in industrial engineering from Arizona State University and a bachelor degree in business administration from Soochow University, Taiwan.

Research

I am broadly interested in mixed-integer programming, network optimization, optimization under uncertainty and their applications. During Ph.D, studies, I worked on a NSF-funded project to develop an mixed-integer optimization model for cascading failure mitigation and restoration on interdependent networks, and a AFRL-funded project to develop exact cutting plane method for least cost influence maximization in social networks.
